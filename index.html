My Story

Coming to America

On the afternoon of August 30th, 2010, I put my first step in America after essentially fleeing from Bangladesh with my parents. Back there our family was institutionally, systematically and religiously targeted by local political goons and government institutions. My tall, towering grandfather, whom I only knew as a man of steel came home weeping like a baby the day this all started to our family. Since then most of our properties were taken from us by either extortion with threats upon our lives or other nefarious and discriminatory means. Fast forwards to today, most of my immediate and extended family members are scattered all over the world, left at different times whenever the opportunities presented itself to them.

My past life before Software Engineering

I finished my last three years of high school here. After, I went to college with a pre-planned life like your typical South Asian young adult. However, in my senior year, as I was counting down my days to graduation, I longed for something else. During this time of self-discovery, I found photography as my hobby and a fondness for politics. However, in terms of career, I still wanted to be in the Tech. industry. Then I recalled the good time I had in an “Intro to C” back in college. So one day I went to code.org and started coding. So that was it - I was hooked. So I began researching the best way to get into the Software Development industry after already graduated from college. The research led me to the world of coding boot camps, and the best and toughest one in New York City revealed to be App Academy. Then I started preparing and preparing, even more, to get into App Academy. Fortunately, the fruits of my labor paid off - I got accepted, survived the next four grueling months of this hardcore program, graduated and became a Software Engineer.

Searching for my next opportunity

In App Academy I did well; well enough to get hired by as an Instructor for their Jumpstart course. In Jumpstart, I lectured on computer science fundamentals and object-oriented programming (particularly Ruby). I also provided personal assistance to students with their problems. Teaching grew on me because I inherently liked helping people. However, coming in, I was not too fond of one aspect of the job - the daily workflow was completely done manually. So I wrote a script in JavaScript to automate the job’s daily workflow. This led my boss to promote me to Technical Coordinator for Jumpstart Online. I began to enjoy this job even more because I was now able to reach students nationwide. However, now I am looking for an opportunity that involves more hands-on development which is the reason that I took the risk to get into this industry in the first place.
